## Generic Transformation

### Before

> This game is amazing. The graphics are stunning and the story is really emotional. I cried at the end. Everyone should play this.

### After

> The game's visual language operates at two registers simultaneously: the photorealistic environmental detail that grounds the player in a recognisable world, and the subtle UI choices that keep the constructed space visible. That tension between immersion and awareness is where the emotional work happens. The ending earns its weight not through manipulation but through the specific accumulation of small choices across forty hours, each one reshaping the player's understanding of what the game has been asking all along.

## English Sample: Mark Fisher, *Capitalist Realism*

I finished Mark Fisher's first 'pamphlet' while travelling. Reading the afterword, I kept picturing him in that small cabin, sorting through scattered notes on the eve of Occupy Wall Street and the Egyptian revolution, and felt an uncontrollable rush of admiration, and of sadness. There is something peculiarly moving about encountering *Capitalist Realism* from the threshold of 2009/2010: before the cracks had fully opened, before anti-capitalism briefly recovered the sense that it might once again speak in the future tense.

The usual objection is that the book lacks philosophical substance, that it names a mood rather than constructs an argument. Fair enough: Fisher is not doing Zizek, and is not trying to. What he is doing, in barely eighty pages, is something rarer: handing an entire generation the vocabulary for what it already felt but could not say. The privatisation of stress. The depoliticisation of mental illness. The way ecological catastrophe gets endlessly 'acknowledged' and then quietly metabolised, as though the acknowledgement itself were the solution. These are not arguments in the academic sense; they are acts of naming, and they land with the force of recognition. The orthodox left will note that Fisher never quite identifies capitalism as a social relation rooted in labour exploitation, that the critique floats as phenomenology unmoored from political economy. There is something to this. But Fisher understood that no amount of correct structural analysis matters if nobody can feel the structure pressing on them, and that making it felt is not ancillary to politics but prerequisite. The very success of the phrase 'capitalist realism' proves the point: it entered the lexicon precisely because it named what was already there, inarticulate and suffocating.

The writing is the decisive thing. Fisher's prose is so compressed and so precise that individual sentences carry the weight of entire chapters elsewhere. That closing line, 'From a situation in which nothing can happen, suddenly anything is possible again', manages to be a political programme and a prayer in the same breath. Knowing now about the unfinished *Acid Communism*, where Fisher was beginning to reframe neoliberalism not as an inevitability but as a counter-revolution against the thinkable, the pamphlet's final pages feel like a coiled spring. By the end I was close to tears. The book solves nothing, and knows it solves nothing; someone had found the words.

### What To Notice

- No em dashes; colons and semicolons carry the rhythm.
- Concession-then-reframe: 'Fair enough... What he is doing... is something rarer.'
- Acts of naming: 'privatisation of stress', 'phenomenology unmoored from political economy'.
- Large vocabulary in controlled sentences.
- Emotion stated plainly at the end.
- Absorbed discourse without survey-like citation.

## Long-Form Reference: *Nothing Is Strange to a Child: On Solenoid*

Treat [_writing/solenoid.md](../_writing/solenoid.md) as the canonical long-form review-essay reference. The notes below describe what the finished essay does and why. They are a checklist for future long-form pieces, not a record of the drafting history.

### Architecture

- The essay opens with a bodily shock — 'Lice came before the fourth dimension' — and lets that image carry the argument before any abstraction arrives. The childhood thesis lands in the second paragraph, not the first. A long essay should give the reader something physical to hold before naming the claim.
- The body is organised as a sequence of category-failures: city/body, fact/invention, biography/counterfactual, mind/body. Each is named explicitly at the head of its paragraph. The sequence escalates so that the deepest dissolution arrives last, not first.
- A single section break separates the category-failures from the formal argument (the solenoid-as-induction). A second section break separates the formal argument from the translator/closing movement. Each break marks a real conceptual move, not a topic change.
- The closing earns its emotional directness by naming what the book did to the reader, not by announcing the book's greatness. 'It gave me a way of suspecting one' is the kind of admission a long admiring essay should reach for in place of adjective catalogues.

### Pull Quotes

- Two pull quotes in the finished essay. Both already exist verbatim in the prose; both are intelligible standing alone; both are line-broken for display rather than minted for the slot.
  - First pull quote: *'One reads Solenoid and begins to distrust one's own furniture.'* This is the architectural premise of the middle of the essay — the reader's own world starts to wobble.
  - Second pull quote: *'Solenoid makes ordinary categories fail, then lets that failure become a way of seeing.'* This is the structural thesis: the formal mechanism (recurrence, induction) is also an ethics of perception.
- Neither pull quote sits adjacent to a section break. The essay never uses two visual hinges for the same turn.
- The body sentence and the displayed pull quote do not sit in the same paragraph. There is enough prose between the body line and the display line that the pull quote feels like a return rather than an echo.

### Reception Handling

- Three outside sources survive in the prose: Cărtărescu's interviews (The Untranslated, Chicago Review, indirectly The Booker Prizes via the source note), Hooyman's labyrinth in the LARB, and Perelmuter's counterfactual reading in Astra.
- Each is metabolised at the point of need. Hooyman appears at the moment the essay needs to ask how the novel holds together; Perelmuter appears in the biography paragraph because the doubled-life reading is what that paragraph needs; the interviews appear where they sharpen a specific claim (Bucharest as alter ego, soma sema as the deepest dissolution).
- No survey paragraph. A reader does not encounter several critics in sequence before the essay has finished exerting its own pressure.

### References And Comparisons

- The only structural reference to another writer in the body is to Cărtărescu's own influences and statements. Pynchon, an early candidate as a structural foil, was cut entirely because the comparison clarified the wrong problem: it framed *Solenoid* as a maximalist totality novel when the essay's actual thesis is about category-dissolution. Remove a comparison whenever it redirects the essay rather than tightening it.
- When an external term enters (soma sema, body-horror, induction), it enters because the prose has already produced the problem the term names. The term arrives as a hinge, not as a frame placed over the work.

### Coil And Drawer As Architectural Call-Backs

- The drawers introduced in the second paragraph ('before dream, memory, shame, illness, hallucination, and fact have been assigned to separate drawers') return in the closing ('after the drawers had been pulled open and emptied onto the floor'). The image carries the essay's whole argument across thousands of words.
- The coil image governs the formal section. Episode coils around episode; failed life around successful life; longing for transcendence around the body that produces the longing. The escalating list lets 'induction rather than ordinary progression' arrive as a discovery, not a summary.
- A useful long-form essay usually has one or two such returns. They should already mean something the first time they appear, and they should mean more on return.

### Translator-Consciousness

- 'I do not read Romanian; my Cărtărescu is therefore necessarily Cotter's Cărtărescu.' The limit is stated plainly before any judgment of the translation is made.
- The translator paragraph commits to a single subject: Cotter's craft. It does not also try to argue the metaphysics of the missing word. A paragraph trying to do two structural jobs at once will tear itself.
- Translator-consciousness is preserved as a personal angle ('I feel this partly as a translator myself'), not promoted into a frame for the whole essay.

### Source Note

- The source note inventories outside sources actually used in the prose, with a single brief mention of the primary edition (Cotter, Deep Vellum, 2022) so the reader knows which translation is under discussion.
- The note distinguishes the 2015 Romanian original from the 2022 English translation because both years carry weight in the prose. When original and translation years both matter, name the relation explicitly.
- Banner and inline image credits live alongside the source note's outside-reading list when they need attribution.

### Common Failure Modes The Essay Avoids

- Opening with the abstraction. The lice come first; the childhood thesis follows.
- Using a pull quote as a section label. Both pull quotes do real work; neither only names the topic of the section it sits in.
- Closing on adjective catalogues. The final paragraph reaches the changed sensorium ('willing, again, to take the strange seriously') without inventory.
- Importing comparisons that are personally available rather than logically necessary. Pynchon was cut for this reason.
- Stacking visual hinges. No pull quote sits beside a section break.
- Loose transitional prose. Each new paragraph names which category is dissolving, or which question forces the next move.

## Long-Form Reference: *Fires a Calculation; Hits a Coordinate: On Gravity's Rainbow*

Treat [_writing/gravitys-rainbow.md](../_writing/gravitys-rainbow.md) as the second canonical long-form reference. It demonstrates skills the *Solenoid* essay does not exercise: a literary argument that depends on technical accuracy, a heavy reception/historical apparatus that must be metabolised at point of need, and a structural thesis (the V-2's double integration) that has to be both formally clean and factually correct.

### Architecture

- The essay opens, like *Solenoid*, with a bodily premise rather than an abstraction: the V-2 arrives before its own sound. The opening movement runs two paragraphs and lands on the diagnostic 'The V-2 does not need hatred at the point of impact. It simply needs procedure.'
- Five movements separated by section breaks: opening (no-warning terror), Brennschluss and the ethics of calculation, Mittelwerk through colonial material to the structural-obscenity claim, Pökler as the System's usable man, Schwarzkommando and the camp inside the spacecraft, and a closing that consolidates paranoia, Mendelson, and the rainbow into a single exhale.
- The closing is one paragraph. An earlier draft used two and read flabby; the merged paragraph runs from 'The rainbow, by the end, cannot console' to 'makes the coordinate answer back' without a paragraph break, and the final image lands harder for it.

### Technical Accuracy As Literary Authority

- The Brennschluss explanation is the essay's structural lever. An early draft framed it as one integration in flight (acceleration → velocity, cutoff on velocity) and a second through ballistic geometry. Pynchon's actual mechanism — and the historical V-2's PIGA-style accelerometer — performs two integrations inside the rocket (acceleration → velocity → distance), with cutoff when accumulated distance equals a target distance set on the ground. Getting this wrong would have undermined the essay's argument that the System hides ethical action *inside* the device.
- The lesson generalises: when an essay's literary argument depends on a piece of technical material — math, biology, engineering — verify the technical claim against a real source. The essay inline-links to Niklas Riewald's math gloss as both citation and reader courtesy.

### Reception And Outside Material Metabolised Inline

- Five external references appear inline in the prose, hyperlinked at the moment of need: Mittelwerk (USHMM), the integrating accelerometer (Riewald), the stupid-dodo myth (NHM), the German-Herero conflict (Britannica), Mendelson's 'Encyclopedic Narrative' (PDF). None of these gets a display-case paragraph; each enters because the surrounding prose has produced the problem the link clarifies.
- The source note opens with Riewald rather than with a 'Read in [edition]' framing. Edition mentions are filler unless edition or translation is materially load-bearing.

### Pull Quotes

- Two pull quotes, both verbatim in the prose, both intelligible standing alone, both line-broken for display.
  - First pull quote: *'The V-2 does not need hatred at the point of impact. It simply needs procedure.'* This lands the *ethics* of the opening, not its optics. An earlier draft used 'A human being disappears into the target area before the rocket ever rises from the launch table' — diagnostically sharp but slightly literary. The cold, two-clause version is better.
  - Second pull quote: *'The System integrates absence over time into obedience.'* Mirrors the rocket's double integration onto Pökler's experience of his daughter; the structural thesis at miniature scale.
- Neither pull quote sits adjacent to a section break. After the second pull quote, the consequence-paragraph ('Love does not stand outside the apparatus as moral exemption. Love can be made into an input.') sits between the display line and the section break that follows.

### The Pökler / Cinema / Frame-by-Frame Insight

- The Pökler section's central insight is that Peenemünde engineers studied rockets through high-speed cinema, splicing test launches into still frames so combustion, trajectory, and failure could be reconstructed across the gaps between exposures. Pökler is then handed his daughter under the same regime — yearly frames, too far apart for biography — and the engineering mind that builds rockets out of discontinuous evidence is asked to build a daughter the same way. The cruelty is that the System has not asked him to believe anything new; it has reused the training.
- This is the kind of reading the essay exists to provide. A Pynchon essay that does not connect cinema to rocket telemetry to fatherhood under sparse data is leaving the most powerful structural rhyme in the novel on the table.

### Capitalised Work-Internal Terms

- The essay capitalises **System** because Pynchon does. Same with **Schwarzkommando**, **Picketists**-style proper nouns, **They** when used as the novel's collective designation. When a novel deploys a load-bearing capitalised noun, follow that capitalisation rather than normalising it.

### Common Failure Modes The Essay Avoids

- Self-credentialing. An early draft opened the calculation paragraph with 'My own work makes me resistant to an easy anti-calculation reading'; the published version drops the autobiography and lets the list (spike train → firing rate, trajectory → variable, populations → rows in a table) carry the same observation in third-person.
- Meta-claim openers. 'Franz Pökler matters because he is frighteningly usable' became 'Franz Pökler is the System's frighteningly usable man.' '\"Ilse, some Ilse\" hurts because it is not scepticism' became '\"Ilse, some Ilse\" is not scepticism.' Drop the announcement; let the next sentence land directly.
- Big abstract nouns. 'For people made discontinuous by empire' became 'by colonialism' — the claim is specifically about colonialism's discontinuities, not about empire as an abstraction.
- Stacked-clause sentences. The Mittelwerk paragraph originally piled architectural facts plus a list plus a colon plus a thesis into one breath; the rewrite breaks the stack into a sequence of short sentences, each carrying one move.
- Repetition tics. The closing originally had 'refusal' five times in fourteen lines; the audit cut three of the five. Before publishing, grep for any noun or verb appearing 3+ times in adjacent paragraphs.
- Two-paragraph closings. Earlier drafts had a 'By the end, the rainbow cannot console' paragraph followed by a separate '*Gravity's Rainbow* offers no clean refusal' paragraph; the merged single paragraph carries the close better.

## Journal-Mode Reference: *Shanwei*

Treat [_writing/shanwei.md](../_writing/shanwei.md) as the canonical journal-mode reference. Journal entries follow different rules from review-essays: they accumulate rather than argue. The notes below describe what *Shanwei* actually does.

### How Journal Mode Differs From Review Mode

- A review-essay drives toward an argument; a journal entry accumulates registers around an emotional centre. Shanwei moves through bodily detail, place memory, family and language, and finally change. None of those movements is argued; each is shown.
- Transitions can be paratactic. The journal does not need to justify why the next paragraph follows the previous one. The fourth paragraph of *Shanwei* pivots from the sea to the Hakka–Teochew language divide without a connective sentence; the pivot earns itself because the entry is not arguing toward a thesis.
- The emotional centre is named once, plainly, and made the pull quote. *'This sea, this sea will not change.'* The line works because the preceding paragraphs have produced everything that *can* change — the cousins graduating, the hotels rising, his own slow lips chasing Teochew phonemes.

### What To Notice In Shanwei

- The opening shock is small and physical: 'The shark meat was in my mouth before I remembered that sharks are cartilaginous fish.' Like the lice in the *Solenoid* opening, the bodily detail comes before any reflective frame, but in journal mode the detail does not have to bear the weight of an argument. It simply opens the door.
- The third paragraph permits a wider claim only because the comparison is concrete: Brighton's pebbles confused the writer because Shanwei's sand and waves had defined what 'sea' meant. Abstraction in journal mode should always be earned by an image the reader can hold.
- The Teochew/Hakka section uses one specific anecdote — the wáng bā joke — to carry both humour and tenderness. Specific dialect markers and a single small scene do more emotional work than a general statement about family or distance ever could.
- The closing paragraph admits open futures ('Nobody knows what this place will look like in ten years') and lands on the one constant. Journal closings should not pretend to resolve.

### Code-Switching In Journal Mode

- *Shanwei* is an English version of a Chinese original; specific Chinese-language elements survive untranslated where translation would erase the texture: dialect names (Hakka, Teochew), the phrase wáng bā in pinyin italics, specific local place names (Lighthouse Island, Red Bay).
- The rule is preservation of texture, not display of bilingualism. A word stays in the original language when translating it would flatten what it means inside the family or the place.

### Pull Quote In Journal Mode

- One pull quote, taken almost verbatim from the closing paragraph: *'This sea, this sea will not change.'* It is the entry's emotional centre, not a structural hinge. Journal pull quotes can be more lyrical and less argumentative than review-essay pull quotes; what they cannot be is invented for the slot.

### Emotion In Journal Mode

- 'This sea is the softest place in my heart' is the kind of sentence that would be unearned in a review-essay. It is earned here because the preceding paragraphs have grounded it in specific water, specific waves, specific dunes, and a specific Brighton confusion. Journal mode allows direct emotional statement when the surrounding detail has done the grounding.

## Chinese Sample Notes

Chinese examples should be taken from the live review corpus when possible, especially:

- Long analytical game reviews with explicit section architecture.
- Lyrical reviews built through repetition and image accumulation.
- Short Douban book reviews with compact pivots, code-switching, and wry closing notes.

What to notice:

- Analytical mode: systematic structure, claims supported by specific mechanics, clear section logic.
- Lyrical mode: parallelism, repetition, and image accumulation carry the argument.
- Book-review mode: compressed, no headers, strong pivot vocabulary, concise closing.
- Code-switching: English terms sit naturally inside Chinese syntax when they preserve precision or tone.
- Emotional force comes from structure, not adjective accumulation.
