3. body text — monospace

Long-form text from the thoughts page (Moon Palace review) in each monospace candidate. Set at the site's actual body size and line-height.

Mononoki current

For all my dissatisfaction with how Auster writes several of the female characters in Moon Palace (especially Kitty Wu and Mrs Hume), I still cannot resist the novel's startling literariness. This is one of those books where Auster's machinery of chance, drift, and sudden revelation finally acquires real grace. The plot keeps turning almost by accident, as if the novel were discovering its own destiny in passing, and one keeps reading in a kind of daze, half for the story and half for the pleasure of how casually it is told.

What I love most is the way the book lets deprivation, wandering, and dependency exist without sinking into self-pity. Marco Fogg and his uncle's life together is precarious, but never miserable in the usual literary way.

EB Garamond for literary text

For all my dissatisfaction with how Auster writes several of the female characters in Moon Palace (especially Kitty Wu and Mrs Hume), I still cannot resist the novel's startling literariness. This is one of those books where Auster's machinery of chance, drift, and sudden revelation finally acquires real grace. The plot keeps turning almost by accident, as if the novel were discovering its own destiny in passing, and one keeps reading in a kind of daze, half for the story and half for the pleasure of how casually it is told.

What I love most is the way the book lets deprivation, wandering, and dependency exist without sinking into self-pity. The same goes for Fogg's homelessness in Central Park, which Auster writes with a strange blend of material exactness and metaphysical lightness.

Natural choice for literary reviews. The old-style figures and classical proportions give weight to criticism.
